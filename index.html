Teaching

Past Syllabus, handouts, and sample syllabi

I am committed to teaching excellence. I actively look for new teaching materials and ways to improve my teaching methods. At UCSB, I have taken several teaching workshops, and prior to this, I participated in a special research project, in partnership with the Federal University of Paran√° (Brazil), aimed at developing new teaching strategies to increase student engagement and success in philosophy courses.

Aside from teaching the relevant material for the class, I am particularly interested in assisting students to develop basic research abilities, written and presentation skills that they can apply to virtually any job in any field.

In my lectures I take a student-centered approach to teaching, where students have an active role in my classes. My most successful strategy to motivate and promote student participation is, by far, the handouts I developed for my lectures. They typically include a list of the main questions that will be addressed in lecture, with space left for student to write down their answers. The handouts facilitate understanding of the material and, as a result, students think and write clearly, and engage with the material in meaningful ways.
