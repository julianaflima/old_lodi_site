CT 1072 - Critical Thinking Seminar in Philosophy of Mind

In this course you will learn how to write a clear and coherent research paper, analyze arguments, some common fallacies, and to use thought experiments in arguments by reading ad critically discussing philosophy papers. You will also develop presentation skills through assignments that you will ask to give a presentation. The topic of the course is on the content of our thoughts (beliefs, desires, hopes) and whether there is a special class of thoughts that have the power to motivate action.

PHI 2300 - Philosophy of Language

This course is an introduction to some traditional and recent topics in Philosophy of Language. We will discuss classical texts form philosophers of the Western Tradition, such as Frege, Russell, Donnellan, etc., about how we use language to talk about the world. We will also touch on pragmatic issues about how we use sentences to communicate information beyond its meaning: when you politely decline an invitation to a party by using the sentence ‘I have to work’, you literally say that you have to work, but often times what you communicate is that you are not going to the party; how is it possible? Lastly, we will discuss theories about the derogatory capacity of pejorative words of phrases, like slurs, insults, etc.
